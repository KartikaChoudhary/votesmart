The Election Journey

Every Indian election follows this official process — from the first announcement to the oath ceremony.

The Election Commission of India (ECI) is an independent constitutional body that conducts all elections. It operates without government interference and its decisions are binding on all parties and candidates.

📢
ECI Announces
Election Announcement & Schedule
Day 0 — The starting gun
  • Chief Election Commissioner holds a press conference to announce election dates
  • Multiple phase dates are declared — India often has 5-7 voting phases for Lok Sabha
  • Model Code of Conduct (MCC) kicks in immediately from this moment
📜
Model Code of Conduct
MCC — Rules Everyone Must Follow
Active from announcement until results
  • Government cannot announce new schemes, launch projects, or transfer key officials
  • Parties cannot use government vehicles, buildings, or resources for campaigning
  • No communal or caste-based vote appeals are allowed
  • Any violation can be reported to ECI — they can censure or disqualify candidates
📋
ECI Process
Notification & Candidate Nominations
~4 weeks before voting
  • Official gazette notification issued for each constituency — election process formally begins
  • Candidates file nomination papers with Returning Officer, pay security deposit
  • Criminal records and assets of ALL candidates are made public on ECI website
  • Scrutiny of nominations — invalid ones are rejected
  • Last date for withdrawal — candidates can pull out, final list published
📣
Parties & Candidates
Election Campaign Period
~3 weeks before voting
  • Parties hold rallies, door-to-door campaigns, distribute manifestos
  • Each candidate has a spending limit — exceeding it is a criminal offence
  • Campaigning must STOP 48 hours before voting — called the Silent Period
  • No exit polls can be published until the last phase of voting ends
🗳️
Voting Day
Polling Day — Democracy in Action
7 AM to 6 PM (varies by state)
  • Voters go to their assigned polling booth with valid ID
  • Name verified → indelible ink on finger → vote on EVM
  • VVPAT shows a paper slip of your vote for 7 seconds — verify it
  • Central Armed Police Forces deployed for security at booths
  • EVMs are sealed and stored securely under multi-party supervision
🔢
Counting
Counting Day & Results
1–2 weeks after last voting phase
  • EVMs brought to counting centres, opened in presence of candidates' agents
  • Counting happens round by round — each round counts one table of EVMs
  • First Past the Post — candidate with highest votes wins the seat
  • For Lok Sabha: 272+ seats needed to claim majority and form government
🏛️
Government Formation
Government Formation & Oath
Within 2–4 weeks of results
  • Winning party/coalition leader invited by President to form government
  • Prime Minister takes oath at Rashtrapati Bhavan, then selects Cabinet
  • If no majority: coalition negotiations begin — can take days or weeks
  • Floor test in Lok Sabha proves the government has the numbers to survive

How to Vote — Step by Step

Follow each step to vote successfully on election day.

📋
Before Election Day
Prepare these things 1–2 days in advance
  • 1Check your name at voters.eci.gov.in or call 1950 (free)
  • 2Note your polling booth address from your Voter ID or ECI website
  • 3Keep valid ID ready: Voter ID, Aadhaar, Passport, PAN, Driving Licence
  • 4Voting is 7 AM – 6 PM. Go early to avoid long queues
💡 Tip: Download the Voter Helpline App from Play Store. Check registration, find your booth, and download your e-EPIC digital Voter ID — all in one place.
🏫
Arrive at Your Polling Booth
Make sure you are at the RIGHT booth
  • 1Booths are usually at schools, community halls, or govt buildings
  • 2Do NOT take phone inside the voting compartment
  • 3Separate queues for men, women, and senior/disabled voters
  • 4Senior citizens (80+) and PwD can apply for home voting
✍️
Verification & Ink Mark
This step proves you are a registered voter
  • 1Show ID to Presiding Officer — they find you in the voter roll
  • 2Sign or thumb-print in Register 17A — records that you voted
  • 3Indelible ink applied to LEFT index finger — won't wash off for days
  • 4You receive a voter slip — take it to the EVM officer inside
ℹ️ If your name is on the list but you are refused, complain to the Presiding Officer on Form 49MA. You have the legal right to vote.
🖲️
Voting on the EVM
EVM = Electronic Voting Machine. Simple, secure, one press.
  • 1EVM shows all candidate names with party symbols and serial numbers
  • 2Press the BLUE button next to your chosen candidate
  • 3A beep confirms your vote is recorded — only ONE vote per voter
  • 4NOTA is the last option if you reject all candidates
🔒 EVMs have no internet, WiFi, or Bluetooth. Remote hacking is impossible. The Supreme Court has upheld EVM integrity multiple times.
🧾
VVPAT — Verify Your Vote
VVPAT = Voter Verified Paper Audit Trail
  • 1After pressing EVM, a paper slip prints in the VVPAT glass box
  • 2Slip shows candidate name, party name, and symbol — visible for 7 seconds
  • 3Slip automatically drops into sealed box — you cannot remove it
  • 4If slip doesn't match what you voted, raise it with Presiding Officer
🎉
You Have Voted!
You have done your democratic duty. Here's what happens next.
  • ✓The ink on your finger proves you voted — stays for 3–5 days
  • ✓EVMs sealed and signed by polling agents of all parties
  • ✓EVMs stored in strong rooms with CCTV and armed guards until counting
  • ✓Results announced on counting day — follow ECI website for live updates
🇮🇳 Your vote decides who represents your constituency in Parliament for the next 5 years.

    Voter Registration & Common Myths

    📞 1950
    National Voter Helpline — Free call, all languages, 8 AM–8 PM
    ✅ How to Register as a Voter
    1
    Check if you're already registered
    Go to voters.eci.gov.in → Search by name + date of birth + state. If found, you're registered. If not, continue.
    2
    Fill Form 6 online (new voters)
    Visit voters.eci.gov.in → New Registration → Form 6. You need: Aadhaar number, address proof, photo. Takes 5 minutes.
    3
    Download the Voter Helpline App
    Search "Voter Helpline" on Play Store or App Store. Register, track application, download e-EPIC digital Voter ID — all in one app.
    4
    Wait for verification (7–30 days)
    A Booth Level Officer (BLO) may visit your address. You'll get SMS confirmation when approved.
    5
    Who is eligible?
    Indian citizen, 18+ years old, resident of the area you're registering in. NRIs can register via Form 6A at the Indian Embassy.
    🔍 Election Myths vs Facts — Tap to reveal
    "EVM machines can be hacked or tampered remotely"FALSE
    • EVMs are standalone — zero internet, WiFi, or Bluetooth connectivity
    • Remote hacking is physically impossible — no signal receiver exists inside
    • VVPAT paper trail enables physical verification of any disputed vote
    • Supreme Court has upheld EVM integrity multiple times after independent audits
    "My single vote doesn't make a difference"FALSE
    • In 2019, 15 Lok Sabha seats were won by margins under 1,000 votes
    • Several UP 2022 assembly seats were decided by fewer than 500 votes
    • Urban low-turnout lets smaller groups decide outcomes — your absence matters
    • Your vote is 1-in-1.5 lakh in a constituency, not 1-in-96-crore
    "You MUST have Voter ID card to vote — nothing else works"FALSE
    • ECI accepts 12 alternative photo ID documents
    • Aadhaar, Passport, PAN, Driving Licence, MNREGA card all accepted
    • Bank passbook with photo, pension documents also valid
    • Your name must be on voter list — ID is only for identity proof at booth
    "NOTA votes go to the winning party"FALSE
    • NOTA (None of the Above) is a valid choice on the EVM — last button
    • NOTA votes are counted separately and do NOT transfer to any candidate
    • Even if NOTA gets most votes, candidate with highest actual votes wins
    • NOTA is a formal way to register dissatisfaction with all candidates
    "Voting is compulsory — you can be fined for not voting"FALSE
    • Voting is NOT compulsory in India at the national or state level
    • No fine or legal penalty exists for not voting in general elections
    • Gujarat has compulsory local body voting with some exceptions — not national
    "Booth capturing still decides elections today"LARGELY FALSE TODAY
    • Booth capturing was serious in 1970s–80s but EVMs made mass stuffing nearly impossible
    • EVM can only record one vote every 5 seconds — rapid stuffing is physically blocked
    • Vote buying still happens — report it on 1950 or CVigil app with photo/video
    • CAPF deployment and webcasting of booths has drastically reduced incidents
